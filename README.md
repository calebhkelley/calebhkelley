## About Me

I'm a data scientist and computational linguist with a Ph.D. in Romance Languages from The University of Alabama. My research background is in NLP, statistical modeling, and large-scale text and speech analysis. I build end-to-end data pipelines, train ML models, and turn unstructured data into actionable findings.

Currently a Visiting Assistant Professor at Emory University, where I teach Spanish linguistics and apply computational methods to research on language variation and identity.

## Featured Project

### [retail-review-nlp](https://github.com/calebhkelley/retail-review-nlp)
NLP analysis of 568K Amazon product reviews. Sentiment analysis (VADER), LDA topic modeling, and supervised classification (Logistic Regression, Random Forest) to extract signals from unstructured customer feedback. Built with Python, scikit-learn, NLTK, and matplotlib.

## Research

My academic work sits at the intersection of computational methods and sociolinguistics. I investigate how phonetic variation encodes social meaning, with a focus on gender, sexuality, and cultural identity in Spanish-speaking communities.

**Areas**: Variationist sociolinguistics, Hispanic phonetics, computational approaches to language variation, second language acquisition, educational technology
